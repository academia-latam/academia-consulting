What we do

Four ways we help institutions get AI right.

Each engagement is grounded in classroom evidence and global standards, then adapted to your faculty, your students, and your context.

Faculty AI training

Hands-on programs that help professors use AI to teach better, not to cut corners, with clear guidance on privacy, bias, and academic integrity.

Curriculum design

AI and data courses, assessments, and labs designed around real learning outcomes, drawing on work built for DeepLearning.AI and Coursera.

AI-literacy courses

Ready-to-run, bilingual courses such as ai-for-teachers, aligned to the UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Teachers (2024).

AI policy & governance advisory

Practical, rights-based guidance for institutions writing their own responsible-AI policies, informed by the EU AI Act, the NIST AI RMF, and UNESCO instruments.

Who we are

An AI-in-education practice with a public-good compass.

AcademIA was founded on a simple conviction: the institutions and teachers who most need help using AI responsibly are often the last to get it. We exist to close that gap, working with universities and schools to make AI adoption equitable, evidence-based, and accountable to students.

Our work bridges three worlds that rarely meet in one place: building AI systems, teaching them, and governing them. That combination is what lets us move from a classroom problem to a policy that actually protects learners.
