# What teachers actually worry about with AI (research digest, 2023–2026)

Source-grounded mini-research used to write the AcademIA copy. Full sources at bottom.

## Ranked concerns (with the angle AcademIA uses)
1. **Students stop thinking / deskilling** — 95% of college faculty fear over-reliance + diminished critical thinking (AAC&U 2025); DEC 2025: 83% worry students can't evaluate AI output. → *Angle:* thinking-forward design; AI becomes the draft the student must defend.
2. **Integrity + the detector trap** — AI detectors are ~50% wrong and disproportionately flag non-native speakers (Markup, Bloomberg). → *Angle:* drop detectors; prove the thinking with process (oral defense, drafts, reflection).
3. **Assessments don't work** — 54% of faculty say evaluation methods are no longer adequate (DEC 2025). → *Angle:* redesign ONE real assessment this term, not a 40-page rubric.
4. **More work, not less** — 34% say AI increased workload vs 8% decreased (Tyton Time for Class 2025). → *Angle:* honest workload curve; grunt-work time-back first.
5. **No training / left behind (MEXICO acute)** — 93% of MX teachers use AI but only 36% teach it; training budget down ~90% since 2016 (ENIAG/SEP via Expansión 2026). DEC 2025: only 6% feel their institution gave enough AI-literacy support. → *Angle (home turf):* train in the teacher's own subject, with their materials, shame-free. "Lo difícil ya lo sabes hacer."
6. **No rules/policy** — only 23% of institutions have an AI acceptable-use policy (EDUCAUSE). → *Angle:* plain-language policy adopt-Monday, written from real cases.
7. **Misinformation / bias / data privacy** — EDUCAUSE: 91% misinformation, 90% data-use-without-consent. → *Angle:* trust-but-verify + which tools NOT to use.
8. **Equity gap (MEXICO acute)** — faster uptake in advantaged schools; infra/funding gaps. → *Angle:* free-tier-first, works on the teacher's phone.
9. **Loss of meaning / role anxiety** — 25% of K-12 (35% HS) say AI does more harm than good (Pew). → *Angle:* protect the human relationship; "AI writes the draft; you make the difference."
